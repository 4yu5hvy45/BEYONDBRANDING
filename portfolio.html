Government · Dubai

Dubai Media Office

Designing for millions means designing responsibly. When communication represents a government institution, clarity becomes more important than creativity.

Social Content · Stage Branding · Design Collaterals
The Challenge
Every announcement, campaign and event asset needed to communicate instantly across different languages, audiences and platforms—while staying consistent with an identity that was already established, not ours to reinvent.
The Thinking
The temptation with public-sector work is to make it interesting. We resisted that. The real work was asking what a viewer needed to understand in the two seconds they'd actually give it.
The Solution
Bilingual campaign systems, stage branding and social templates built around a strict hierarchy—one idea per frame, repeated until it felt familiar rather than novel.
The Outcome
Materials that held together across dozens of touchpoints and multiple teams, without a single style guide meeting.
What I Learned
Good public communication isn't remembered because it's beautiful. It's remembered because people never had to think twice.
Hospitality

Marriott Indore

Hospitality doesn't end at the hotel entrance. During the pandemic, the guest experience had to travel home with them.

Packaging · Campaign Design · Guest Collateral
The Challenge
Luxury hotels needed to deliver the same level of care through takeaway packaging, festive hampers and communication that guests would normally only experience inside the property.
The Thinking
The challenge wasn't designing packaging. It was preserving the feeling of Marriott outside the hotel—a much harder brief than it sounds.
The Solution
Every detail, from materials to typography, was considered so the experience remained recognisably Marriott the moment it left the building.
The Outcome
Packaging and collateral that guests associated with the same quality as the in-hotel experience, delivered through a fraction of the usual touchpoints.
What I Learned
Premium brands don't become memorable because they look expensive. They become memorable because every touchpoint feels intentional.
Luxury Retail

Valdaria

Building a brand before building awareness. Valdaria wasn't looking for another logo. It needed an identity that could stand beside established luxury suiting brands.

Brand Identity · Exhibition Design · Event Graphics
The Challenge
A brand with no market awareness, entering a category where competitors had decades of trust already built in.
The Thinking
Building a brand before building awareness meant every asset had to do the work a reputation usually does.
The Solution
The work extended far beyond visual identity—exhibition spaces, printed material and fabric presentation, all designed to create confidence before the first conversation began.
The Outcome
A presence that read as established from day one, in a category that punishes anything that looks new.
What I Learned
Luxury isn't decoration. Luxury is restraint.
Luxury Retail

Manek

Trust begins before the fabric is touched. When customers invest in premium shirting, their first impression often happens long before they experience the product itself.

Brand Identity · Packaging · Sales Collateral
The Challenge
Trust needed to begin before the product was in anyone's hands—through packaging, catalogues and sales collateral alone.
The Thinking
Branding doesn't begin with the product. It begins with expectation. We designed for the moment before the sale, not the moment of it.
The Solution
Packaging, catalogues, sales collateral and exhibition material became part of one continuous experience, rather than separate deliverables handled in isolation.
The Outcome
A retail presence that carried the same confidence at every stage, from shelf to store to sale.
What I Learned
The first impression is rarely the product itself. It's everything that surrounds it.
Packaging

Indian Kawfee Co.

Packaging should tell a story before the first sip. Coffee isn't only judged by taste. It's judged on the shelf, in the seconds before anyone tastes anything.

Brand Identity · Packaging Design
The Challenge
Celebrate Indian coffee culture without leaning on the clichés the category usually reaches for.
The Thinking
The objective wasn't louder packaging. It was packaging people wanted to pick up—a quieter, harder goal to design for.
The Solution
Every visual decision balanced authenticity with contemporary retail appeal, so the identity felt rooted without feeling dated.
The Outcome
Packaging that stood out on shelf without shouting, and that customers described as feeling authentically Indian.
What I Learned
Story sells before flavour does. Give people something to believe before they taste anything.
What Changed

What these projects taught me.

Every project leaves something behind. Marriott reinforced the importance of consistency. Government work reminded me that clarity always beats decoration. Retail brands proved that first impressions often decide the sale.

Those lessons quietly shape every project that follows.
